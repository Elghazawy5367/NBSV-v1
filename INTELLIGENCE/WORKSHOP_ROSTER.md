## HOW TO PRE-QUALIFY A WORKSHOP

Before any real order, give the workshop a low-stakes test job:

> "Make 5 units of [simple basic item] in 3 days at [X EGP/unit]."

Evaluate on:
1. Quality of finish (does it match the spec?)
2. Adherence to timeline (did they deliver when promised?)
3. Communication (did they ask good clarifying questions?)
4. Pricing honesty (did the final price match the quote?)

**Pass** = qualified partner. **Fail** = drop before you depend on them under a season deadline.

---

## THE WORKSHOP AS PASSIVE SCOUT

After establishing a working relationship, add this once:

> "If any customer ever brings you something to copy that you've never seen before — something that looks new or clever — send me a photo. If I end up producing it, you get the first batch order."

Workshop owners see products from hundreds of clients. They observe what's being made before anyone sells it. This turns them into zero-cost intelligence sensors at no cost to activate.
