## THE FUNDAMENTAL DIFFERENCE FROM ALL OTHER SOURCES

**The product does not exist anywhere on earth yet.**

Not in China. Not on Kickstarter. Not in any concept library. You originated it in collaboration with AI. There is no global reference price. No AliExpress listing coming. No closing timing window. No Chinese supplier count to race against.

**You set the clock.**

The copycat still exists — but they are copying your original physical product. There is no 1688 schematic available to 40 competitors simultaneously. The reverse-engineering window starts when your product appears in the first shop. Not before.

**The defense on AI-originated products is deeper than timing alone:**
- Pre-sell moat (shop commitments before production)
- Zone exclusivity (geographic protection with each shop)
- Iteration speed (you can evolve the product faster than a copycat can copy it)
- Semi-DIY finish differentiation (the finish layer changes seasonally — impossible to copy without knowing the system)

## THE PROMPT IS THE SKILL

Generic prompts produce generic outputs. The constraints you inject are your competitive edge — because only you know:
- The Egyptian workshop material reality
- The WTP ceiling for Egyptian buyers
- The specific AHA threshold required for shelf self-selling
- The specific Egyptian daily behaviors that create real demand

**The tighter the buyer description and the pain description, the more useful the output.**

❌ "Egyptian mother" → noise
✅ "Cairo mother of 3 children aged 6, 9, and 12 living in a 75sqm apartment managing 3 school bags, 2 sports bags, and cleaning supplies at the entrance door every morning" → candidates

## THE MASTER IDEATION PROMPT
### Copy-paste this entire block at the start of every AI ideation session

```
I am a solo Egyptian founder running a physical product business.
I need product concepts that can be locally manufactured and sold 
in Egypt with zero personal capital at risk.

Generate [N] product concepts for:

BUYER: [Describe a specific Egyptian person in detail — age, 
location, living situation, family structure]

PAIN: [Describe a specific daily frustration this person 
experiences — be as concrete and behavioral as possible]

EVERY concept must satisfy ALL of the following constraints 
with absolutely zero exceptions:

─── PRODUCTION CONSTRAINTS ────────────────────────────────
Made ONLY from these materials (Egyptian workshop sources):
• MDF / plywood / solid wood
• Acrylic / perspex sheet  
• Aluminum sheet (locally produced in Egypt)
• Standard fabric / textile / canvas
• Foam / sponge / EVA sheet
• Standard hardware (screws, hinges, brackets, tubes, knobs)
• Heat press / screen print on standard blanks
• Basic resin or epoxy poured elements

ZERO electronics of any kind (no LED, no battery, no USB, 
no sensor, no motor — nothing powered)
ZERO injection molding
ZERO imported core components
Maximum 6 assembly steps
Producible by a small Egyptian workshop with basic tools in 1–2 days
One sentence to describe manufacture to a workshop owner who 
has never heard of this product

─── ECONOMICS CONSTRAINTS ─────────────────────────────────
COGS under 80 EGP at batch 30 (standard path)
OR under 120 EGP if a home finish layer (sanding, staining, 
felt lining, painting, hardware addition) adds 30%+ perceived 
value and takes 20 minutes or less per unit

─── AHA CONSTRAINTS ────────────────────────────────────────
A stranger must stop and pick this up within 3 seconds 
of seeing it sitting on a shop counter with no signage
Zero explanation needed — the value is instantly visible
AHA must be emotional and visual (desire, "I need this", 
"I know who to give this to") — NOT intellectual
("that's clever" without "I want it" is not enough)

─── MARKET CONSTRAINTS ─────────────────────────────────────
Must NOT exist anywhere in Egypt:
not on Jumia, not on Noon, not on Facebook Marketplace,
not on Instagram Egypt, not in any physical shop

─── EGYPTIAN FIT CONSTRAINT ────────────────────────────────
Must replace or enhance a SPECIFIC Egyptian daily behavior
Not globally generic — must connect to Egyptian reality:
Cairo apartment sizes (avg 60–90 sqm), Cairo traffic culture,
Egyptian gifting obligations, Egyptian hosting culture,
Egyptian investment in children's education

─── OUTPUT FORMAT ──────────────────────────────────────────
For each concept output EXACTLY this structure:

**Product Name:** (3 words maximum)

**What it is:** (One sentence — no jargon)

**How it is made:** (One sentence to a workshop owner who 
has never heard of this product)

**The AHA moment:** (What does a stranger see and feel 
in the first 3 seconds?)

**Egyptian behavior it replaces or enhances:** 
(Name the specific behavior)

**Materials:** (Egyptian sources only — be specific)

**COGS estimate at batch 30:** (EGP)

**Why no Egyptian has seen this before:**

**Production path:** Standard Workshop / Semi-DIY Finish

─── SELF-FILTER INSTRUCTION ────────────────────────────────
Before outputting any concept, check it yourself against 
every constraint above. If it fails any single constraint — 
discard it and generate a replacement. 
Only output concepts that pass every constraint.
Do not apologize for discarded concepts — just replace them.
```

## THE AI TOOLS — WHAT EACH DOES BEST

### Claude (This Project) — Primary Ideation Partner
**Strength:** Deep constraint adherence, systems thinking, consistent output structure

Use Claude first. The project context (KB, filter, Egyptian reality) is already embedded. No need to re-explain the system with every prompt. Just run the Master Prompt.

**Best for:** First-pass ideation session with full constraint set. Filtering evaluation of candidates from other sources. Cross-checking concepts from other AI tools.

---

### Qwen `qwen.alibaba.com`
**Strength:** Heavier Chinese product and manufacturing training data than Western models

Qwen draws on manufacturing knowledge from Alibaba's ecosystem. For storage and organization products specifically, it generates concepts closer to what is actually producible in workshop environments because its training includes Chinese small-batch manufacturing context.

**Best for:** Second opinion on concepts Claude generated. Independent ideation session on the same buyer/pain — compare outputs. Generating concepts in the storage/organization space specifically.

**How to use:** Run the exact same Master Prompt on Qwen. Compare with Claude's output. Concepts that both models independently generate with structural similarity have stronger signal.

---

### Perplexity AI `perplexity.ai`
**Strength:** Real-time web synthesis — surfaces what's discussed NOW

Not a generative ideation tool. Use Perplexity to find live signals that static AI training cannot provide.

**Useful queries:**
```
What simple physical products have gone viral on Douyin 
in the last 30 days in the home organization category?

What are the most upvoted posts on r/somebodymakethis 
in the last 60 days?

What product concepts are people requesting that don't 
exist yet in gift packaging or desk organization?

What new physical products launched on Kickstarter 
in the last 90 days in home goods?
```

---

### ChatGPT `chat.openai.com` (Free Tier)
**Strength:** Different training perspective — occasionally surfaces concepts Claude wouldn't generate

**Best for:** Running the Master Prompt as a third source when Claude and Qwen have both been exhausted. Convergence signal: when three different models independently produce structurally similar concepts, the concept has passed a multi-source AHA test.

---

### Gemini `gemini.google.com`
**Strength:** Google search data embedded in training — stronger signal for search gaps

**Best for:** Identifying products people are searching for but can't find.

**Useful prompt:**
```
What physical products do people commonly search for on 
Google in the home organization, gift packaging, or 
children's education categories that return very few 
results or unsatisfying results? 
Focus on Egypt and Middle East markets.
```

---

### Google NotebookLM `notebooklm.google.com`
**Strength:** Synthesizes uploaded documents to find patterns you haven't seen

**Not for ideation — for pattern recognition.**

Upload the NBSV KB and FAILURE_LOG after 10+ rejection cycles. Query:
```
Based on all the products that have failed in this system 
(listed in the failure log), what product type has never 
been attempted that fits all the production and AHA 
constraints? What patterns appear in the rejections?
```

The failure library becomes a pattern recognition engine when fed to NotebookLM.

## RUNNING A PRODUCTIVE IDEATION SESSION

**Before the session:**
- Open `CANDIDATES/FAILED/FAILURE_LOG.md` — review the last 10 rejections to avoid recycling the same concept types
- Identify the current strongest unmet need you've personally observed in Egypt recently
- Set a specific buyer and a specific pain before opening any AI tool

**During the session:**
- Run the Master Prompt with your buyer/pain specification
- Request 5 concepts minimum — most will fail the self-filter, fewer will reach you
- For any concept that passes initial reading: run the 3-question filter manually before advancing
- Screenshot or copy the concept immediately — do not rely on chat history

**After the session:**
- Every rejected concept gets one line in the FAILURE_LOG with the kill signal
- Every surviving concept gets a CANDIDATES/ACTIVE/ file
- If two or more concepts survive: rank by pre-sell confidence, not personal preference

| Factor | Chinese Platform | Kickstarter | AI Ideation |
