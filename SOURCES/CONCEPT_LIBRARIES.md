# NBSV Source Intelligence — Concept Libraries
## Source Channel 3 — Designed But Never Manufactured
### Open this file before every concept library scan session

---

## THE NATURE OF THIS CHANNEL

No demand has been externally proven here. These are design concepts that exist as images, renders, or portfolio pieces — never produced, never sold, never crowdfunded.

**What this means operationally:**
- The AHA must come from your own judgment reading the design
- The pre-sell step carries more weight for candidates from this source
- The upside: products here have zero global competition — no AliExpress listing exists, no Chinese factory has produced it, no Egyptian importer has seen it
- If the pre-sell passes, you are the first manufacturer on earth

**The AHA question applies differently here:** you are not watching someone else's emotional reaction. You are generating your own. Apply the same standard — would a stranger stop and pick this up from a counter without any explanation? If you have to imagine the explanation — reject.

## PLATFORM 1 — YANKO DESIGN
**URL:** `yankodesign.com`

Industrial design concept publication. Daily new product concepts across all categories.

### What to Look For
Concepts described as: **"simple" / "low-tech" / "materials-focused" / "minimal"**

Avoid concepts described as: "smart" / "connected" / "AI-powered" / "sensor-based" / "app-controlled"

### Most Productive NBSV Categories on Yanko
Browse by tag: `Storage` `Organization` `Everyday Carry` `Gift` `Workspace` `Furniture`

### Navigation
Homepage scroll daily = 5 minutes, high signal density. The most valuable concepts are often the ones that look obvious in hindsight — simple geometry, clever material use, obvious-once-you-see-it combination.

### Time Caution
Concepts published more than 24 months ago may have been manufactured since. If you find a strong candidate from an older post, run Egyptian market verification before spending further time on it.

## PLATFORM 2 — DEZEEN
**URL:** `dezeen.com`

More architecturally focused than Yanko but contains a strong Products section.

### Relevant Sub-sections
`Products → Furniture` / `Products → Homeware` / `Products → Stationery`

Dezeen's editorial standard is higher — fewer concepts, higher quality signal per item reviewed. Concepts producible by an Egyptian workshop are rarer here but when they appear, they're often genuinely novel and have not been surfaced by other scanners.

### Scan Frequency
Weekly is sufficient. Daily would produce very low signal-to-time ratio.

## PLATFORM 3 — CORE77
**URL:** `core77.com`

Industrial design professional community. Mix of professional work, student projects, and editorial commentary.

### Most Valuable Sections for NBSV
- `Design Feed` — daily community-shared projects
- `Projects` — portfolio pieces from professional and student designers
- **Annual Design Awards** (browse previous years): Runner-up and honorable mention products in `Consumer Products` and `Furniture & Lighting` categories are often excellent concepts that found recognition but no manufacturing path

### Why Previous Years' Awards Matter
Award-winning concepts from 2–4 years ago were validated by industry professionals as excellent ideas. Many were never manufactured because the designer had no production or distribution connection. You have both.

## PLATFORM 4 — BEHANCE
**URL:** `behance.net`

Adobe's design portfolio platform. Enormous volume — requires aggressive filtering.

### Navigation to Useful Content
Search filters: Creative Fields → **Industrial Design** / **Product Design**
Sort by: **Appreciated** (most saves/likes from other designers)

### Search Terms That Produce NBSV-Relevant Results
```
product design concept organizer
storage product design
gift packaging concept
workspace organization design
space saving design concept
flat pack furniture design
modular storage design
```

### What to Skip Immediately
- Visual design (logos, branding, UI/UX) — wrong category
- Concept renders that show electronic components
- Designs that require factory tooling (complex injection-molded parts)
- Student projects with no manufacturing consideration (the design is the art, not the product)

## PLATFORM 6 — REDDIT r/SOMEBODYMAKETHIS
**URL:** `reddit.com/r/somebodymakethis`

**This is the most direct unmet-need signal in any concept library.**

Real people describing products they want that do not exist. No design, no render. Pure unmet need in plain language. Posts with 500+ upvotes represent genuine widespread desire for something that literally does not exist anywhere on earth.

### How to Navigate

**Sort by: Top → All Time**
Produces the highest-desire, longest-validated unmet needs. The fact that a post from 3 years ago still has 2,000 upvotes and no product exists means this is a real market gap.

**Sort by: Hot**
Produces recent emerging needs. Less validation but current.

### How to Read a Post for NBSV

```
1. What is the person describing? (translate to product spec)
2. Is this solvable with MDF / acrylic / fabric / aluminum?
3. Is this a universal problem or Egypt-specific? 
   (global demand + Egyptian market clean = ideal)
4. Has anyone in the comments said "this exists at [link]"?
   If yes — skip. Product already exists.
5. Has anyone in the comments started making it?
   If yes — check if it's reached Egypt.
```

### The Key Insight on This Platform
The person who posted had the idea but no manufacturing path. You have the manufacturing path but need the validated idea. This is the most direct possible match between what you need and what this platform provides.

**Constraint:** Posts are global, not Egypt-specific. Apply Egyptian reality check manually.

## PLATFORM 8 — PRODUCT HUNT (Physical Products Section)
**URL:** `producthunt.com` | **Filter:** Tags: Hardware / Maker

Physical products that launch here have often been conceived by someone with no manufacturing path — they built one unit and launched it. Comments are the most valuable part: "I've wanted this for years" or "where has this been my whole life?" = confirmed AHA signal in a global audience.

**Signal threshold:** 500+ upvotes on a simple physical product with no core electronics.

## HOW TO EVALUATE A CONCEPT LIBRARY CANDIDATE

Unlike Asian platforms where you see real demand signals (likes, orders, comments), concept libraries require you to supply the judgment:

**The 4 questions to answer before advancing any concept library candidate:**

1. **"Can I describe exactly how this is made in one sentence to a workshop owner?"**
   If no — reject. A concept you cannot describe is not producible by an Egyptian workshop.

2. **"What does a stranger see in 3 seconds when they look at this?"**
   Describe the 3-second visual experience. If it requires the design description to explain — reject.

3. **"What specific Egyptian behavior does this replace or enhance?"**
   Name the behavior specifically. Not "organization" — "the chaos of managing 3 children's school bags at the apartment door every morning." Vague answer = reject.

4. **"Is there any reason this design requires something an Egyptian workshop cannot make?"**
   Check materials, tolerances, assembly complexity, any specialized tooling. One red-light element = reject.
