### 1A — Chinese Platforms

**Douyin** `douyin.com` — Install APK via APKPure, not international TikTok

The domestic Chinese algorithm is fundamentally different from international TikTok. It surfaces product demo content to non-followers based on engagement velocity. A video with 2M likes on a product you've never seen means millions of strangers stopped their scroll for it. That is the signal.

What to search:
```

家居好物   (Home Good Products)
创意好物   (Creative Good Products)
桌面收纳   (Desk Storage)

创意家居   (Creative Home)
布艺收纳   (Fabric Storage)
礼品包装   (Gift Packaging)
```

What the signal looks like: comment sections full of `在哪买` (where to buy) and `求链接` (send me the link) — desire, not just admiration.

---

**Xiaohongshu** `xiaohongshu.com` — Available on Google Play in some regions, otherwise APK

Different signal from Douyin. Less entertainment, more discovery. Users share products they found and loved. Format is closer to Pinterest with real purchase intent underneath.

**1688** `1688.com` — Mobile browser, no app needed

The wholesale sourcing layer beneath the viral layer. When a product goes viral on Douyin, 1688 suppliers appear within 2–4 weeks. The supplier count is your pipeline clock:

| Supplier Count | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 suppliers | Too early | Hold and monitor weekly |
| 3–8 suppliers | Sweet spot — window open | PROCEED with urgency |
| 10–19 suppliers | Late but possible | Verify AliExpress status immediately |
| 20+ at commodity pricing | Import wave starting | ABORT |

Browse categories:
```
家居用品 → 收纳整理   (Home Goods → Storage & Organization)
礼品 → 节庆用品       (Gifts → Festival Supplies)
文具 → 桌面收纳       (Stationery → Desk Storage)
工艺礼品              (Craft Gifts — for Semi-DIY hardware sourcing)
```

**Taobao** `taobao.com` — Consumer retail layer, different from 1688

Sits between 1688 (wholesale) and Douyin (viral). Products on Taobao with 1,000+ reviews but not yet on AliExpress in English = sweet spot. Use Google Translate camera on tablet to read listings.

---

**Pinduoduo** `pinduoduo.com` — Mass market volume signal ONLY

Do not scan for ideas here. Use only as volume confirmation: if something has 50,000+ monthly orders on Pinduoduo, the Chinese mass market has already validated it. Douyin is where you find it. Pinduoduo is where you confirm it escaped niche into mainstream.

---

**WeChat Channels** — Underused by non-Chinese scanners, advantage window still open

WeChat's video platform surfaces product content to a different demographic than Douyin — older, higher income, more purchasing power. Requires WeChat account (email registration works). Less competition in this channel from other NBSV-style scanners globally.

## SOURCE 2 — VALIDATED CONCEPT MINES
### Never Before Produced — Proven Demand Without Supply

**The structural difference from Source 1:** someone already proved strangers will pay before the product exists. The AHA has been externally validated by real money, not just engagement metrics.

---

**Kickstarter** `kickstarter.com`

Navigate: Discover → Design & Technology → Product Design → Sort by Most Funded or Newly Launched

What you're looking for: Simple physical products (not electronics, not software) with 500+ backers where the innovation is in geometry, material combination, or clever mechanism — not in proprietary technology.

**Three time windows on Kickstarter:**

| Window | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Live campaign, 500+ backers, simple physical product | Demand proven, not yet manufactured at scale | Proceed — strongest possible signal |
| Successfully funded, shipping to backers now (6–12 months ago) | Check Egyptian market | Often still clean — Kickstarter rarely reaches Egypt in first 12–18 months |
| Funded 1–3 years ago, moderate backer count | May have reached Amazon | Verify Egyptian market first — if clean, still valid |

**Kill signal specific to Kickstarter:** campaign raised over $1M AND featured in mainstream tech press (Wired, TechCrunch) = likely heading to mass market. Verify Egyptian market urgently.

---

**Indiegogo** `indiegogo.com`

More permissive than Kickstarter. Apply higher bar: minimum 500 backers AND successfully funded AND physical product with no core electronic component.

Indiegogo "InDemand" section shows products still selling after campaign ended — multi-year validated demand signal.

---

**Makuake** `makuake.com` — Japanese crowdfunding, underused signal

Japan's dominant product crowdfunding platform. Products here are often original Japanese designs with zero English-language coverage. A product with 1,000+ backers on Makuake and no AliExpress equivalent = extremely early window globally.

Browse: `生活・インテリア` (Lifestyle/Interior) and `ホーム` (Home). Google Translate browser extension handles the language.

---

**Green Funding** `greenfunding.jp` — Second Japanese platform

Browse `生活・インテリア` and `アウトドア・スポーツ`. Narrower catalog than Makuake but occasionally surfaces genuinely novel concepts.

---

**Campfire** `camp-fire.jp` — Third Japanese platform

Broader, includes more artisan and niche products. Useful specifically for gift packaging concepts and small-batch organizational products.

---

**WeFunder / Republic** `wefunder.com` `republic.co`

US equity crowdfunding platforms. Physical product companies raising early capital here have validated their concept enough to attract investors. Signal is weaker than Kickstarter backer counts, but product pipeline is often 18–24 months ahead of market.

## SOURCE 3 — CONCEPT LIBRARIES
### Designed But Never Manufactured

**The distinction from Source 2:** no demand has been proven externally. These are design concepts that exist as images, renders, or prototypes — never produced, never sold. The AHA must come from your own judgment reading the design. The Egyptian market pre-sell is the validation.

---

**Yanko Design** `yankodesign.com`

Industrial design concept publication. Daily new product concepts.

What to look for: concepts described as "simple," "low-tech," "materials-focused" — NOT "smart," "connected," or "AI-powered."

Most productive NBSV categories: `Storage` `Organization` `Everyday Carry` `Gift` `Workspace`

Navigation: Browse by category tag or scroll homepage daily (5 minutes, high signal density). Concepts published more than 24 months ago may have been manufactured since — verify before advancing.

---

**Dezeen** `dezeen.com`

More architecturally focused but contains a strong `Products` section.

Relevant sub-sections: `Furniture` `Homeware` `Stationery`

Dezeen's editorial standard is higher — fewer concepts, higher quality signal. Concepts producible by an Egyptian workshop are rarer here but when they appear, they're often genuinely novel.

---

**Core77** `core77.com`

Industrial design professional community. Browse `Design Feed` and `Projects` sections.

Particularly relevant: annual Design Awards — runner-up and honorable mention products are often excellent concepts that never found a manufacturing path. Browse previous years' awards in `Consumer Products` and `Furniture & Lighting` categories.

---

**Behance** `behance.net`

Search: "product design concept" / "storage concept design" / "organizational product" / "gift packaging design"

Narrow using search filters: Creative Fields → Industrial Design / Product Design. Sort by Appreciated (most saves) to surface designs that triggered strongest emotional response.

Volume is enormous — 90% is visual design (logos, branding, UI) rather than physical products. Filter aggressively.

---

**Coroflot** `coroflot.com`

Professional industrial design portfolio platform, less known than Behance but more densely concentrated with physical product work. Designers post work that never went to market because they had no production or distribution path.

Search: `storage` `organizer` `packaging` `workspace`

---

**Reddit r/somebodymakethis** `reddit.com/r/somebodymakethis`

Reverse-direction signal — real people describing products they want that don't exist. No design, no render, no prototype. Pure unmet need in words.

Posts with 500+ upvotes = genuine widespread desire for a product that literally does not exist anywhere.

Sort by: **Top → All Time** (proven high-desire concepts) or **Hot** (recent emerging needs)

Constraint: posts are global, not Egypt-specific. Apply Egyptian reality check manually to every concept.

---

**Pinterest** — Visual signal aggregator

Search: "clever storage ideas" / "gift packaging design" / "desk organization DIY" / "space saving furniture"

Images appearing most frequently across different boards from different users indicate persistent visual desire. When the same concept appears in 50+ different boards — that is an AHA signal validated by thousands of saves without anyone producing it at scale.

## SOURCE 4 — AI IDEATION TOOLS
### Prompt-Originated Concepts — You Are The Conceiver

**The fundamental difference from all other sources:** the product does not exist anywhere. Not in China, not on Kickstarter. You originated it. There is no global reference. No closing timing window. No AliExpress listing coming. You set the clock.

The copycat still exists — but they are copying your original, not reproducing a globally available Chinese schematic. No 1688 supplier is selling your specs to 40 competitors simultaneously.

---

**Claude** (this project) — Primary ideation partner

The prompt is the skill. Constrained prompts produce actionable candidates. Generic prompts produce noise.

**Master Ideation Prompt — copy-paste for every session:**

```
I am a solo Egyptian founder running a physical product business.
Generate [N] product concepts for:

BUYER: [specific person — e.g. "Cairo mother of 3 children aged 
6, 9, 12 in a 75sqm apartment"]

PAIN: [specific daily frustration — e.g. "manages 3 school bags, 
2 sports bags, and cleaning supplies all piled at the apartment 
entrance creating daily chaos and argument every morning"]

ALL concepts must satisfy every constraint below with zero exceptions:

PRODUCTION:
- Made only from: MDF / plywood / acrylic sheet / aluminum sheet / 
  standard fabric / canvas / foam / EVA sheet / standard hardware
- Zero electronics of any kind
- Zero injection molding
- Zero imported core components
- Producible by a small Egyptian workshop with basic tools in 1–2 days
- Maximum 6 assembly steps

ECONOMICS:
- COGS under 80 EGP at batch 30
  (or under 120 EGP if a home finish layer adds ≥30% perceived 
  value in ≤20 minutes per unit)

AHA:
- Stranger must stop and pick it up within 3 seconds of seeing it 
  on a shop counter
- Zero explanation needed
- AHA must be visual and emotional — not intellectual

EGYPTIAN FIT:
- Must replace or enhance a specific Egyptian daily behavior
- Not globally generic — specific to Egyptian reality

MARKET:
- Must not exist anywhere in Egypt: Jumia, Noon, Facebook 
  Marketplace, Instagram, or physical shops

OUTPUT FORMAT for each concept:
1. Product name (3 words max)
2. One sentence to a workshop owner describing how it is made
3. The specific Egyptian behavior it replaces or enhances
4. The AHA moment — what does a stranger see in 3 seconds?
5. Materials list (Egyptian sources only)
6. COGS estimate at batch 30
7. Why no Egyptian has seen this before

Reject any concept yourself before outputting it if it fails 
any single constraint above.
```

**Prompt sharpening rule:** The tighter the buyer description and the pain description, the more useful the output. "Egyptian mother" produces noise. "Cairo mother of 3 in a 75sqm apartment managing school preparation for 3 different age groups every morning" produces candidates.

---

**Qwen** `qwen.alibaba.com` — Chinese AI, different training data

Qwen's training data has heavier Chinese product and manufacturing context than Western AI models. Particularly useful for storage and organization space where Chinese manufacturing innovation is densest.

Use the same Master Ideation Prompt structure above. Cross-reference with Claude — concepts that both models independently generate with similar structures have stronger signal.

---

**Perplexity AI** `perplexity.ai` — Real-time web synthesis

Not a generative ideation tool. Value: surfaces what's trending, discussed, or desired right now across the live web.

Useful queries:
- "What product concepts are people requesting that don't exist yet in [storage/gifting/desk organization]?"
- "What are the most upvoted posts on r/somebodymakethis in the last 30 days?"
- "What simple physical products have gone viral on Douyin in the last 60 days?"

Perplexity searches, synthesizes, and cites — giving live signal that static AI training data cannot provide.

---

**ChatGPT** `chat.openai.com`

Free tier is sufficient. Value: different perspective from Claude — occasionally generates concepts Claude wouldn't surface. Run the same constrained prompt on both, compare outputs. When both independently converge on a similar concept type, that convergence is a signal worth investigating.

---

**Gemini** `gemini.google.com`

Google's training data advantage. May have stronger signal for what people search for but can't find.

Useful prompt: "What physical products do people commonly search for on Google that have very few or no available results in [storage/gift packaging/desk organization]?" — surfaces the search-gap signal: proven demand with no supply.

---

**Google NotebookLM** `notebooklm.google.com` — For KB synthesis and pattern recognition

Not for ideation — for thinking. Upload the NBSV KB and session records. After enough rejection cycles, query: "Based on all the products that have failed in this system, what product type has never been attempted that fits all the constraints?" The failure library fed into NotebookLM becomes a pattern recognition engine.

---

**Product Hunt** `producthunt.com`

Browse the `Hardware` and `Maker` tags. Physical products that launch here have often been conceived by someone with no manufacturing path. Comments saying "I've wanted this for years" or "where has this been?" = AHA signal that has never reached Egyptian awareness.

## SOURCE 5 — GITHUB EXPLOITATION
### The Technical Layer You Access Without Being Technical

GitHub is not a code repository for this purpose. It is an intelligence layer — where product ideas, automation logic, research aggregations, and community-built tools live that no mainstream platform surfaces.

**The zero-technical-skill access:** Every file in every public GitHub repository is readable in a browser. Navigate to the repository, click any file, read it. No account required for reading. No technical knowledge required. GitHub is a library. You are a reader.

---

### 5A — What GitHub Actually Contains That's Useful

**Awesome Lists — Community-curated directories of everything**

Search GitHub for:
```
awesome product design
awesome industrial design
awesome manufacturing
awesome crowdfunding
awesome laser cutting
```

These are community-maintained lists of resources, platforms, tools, and websites organized by category. They contain sources that never appear in any Google search — niche platforms, regional databases, specialized communities. A single "awesome list" in the right domain can surface 20 new scanning sources.

---

**Product Research Repositories — Publicly shared research**

Individual researchers and product developers publish market research, trend analysis, and product gap studies as public GitHub repositories.

Search:
```
product opportunity research
market gap analysis
unmet needs physical product
product ideas 2025
trending products analysis
```

These are often thesis projects, side project research, or professional analyses shared publicly. The person who did the research had no manufacturing path. You do.

---

**Trend Analysis Tools Built by Others — Use without building**

Search:
```
trending products scraper
aliexpress trending
product trend analysis
taobao trending products
```

Developers have built open-source tools that automatically track trending products across AliExpress, Amazon, and other platforms. Repositories often contain output data files — browse the results directly. You don't need to run the code. You read the output.

---

**Design System Repositories — Parametric and modular designs**

Search:
```
parametric furniture design
laser cut design files organizer
CNC furniture plans
flat pack furniture design
laser cut storage
svg design files home
```

Designers publish parametric design files (SVG, DXF, AI formats) for products they've designed but never manufactured commercially. These are ready-to-produce files — hand one to an Egyptian laser cutting workshop and they can produce it the same day.

Designs with 100+ GitHub stars have been validated as interesting by hundreds of other designers globally.

### 5D — The Four Functions of the Repository

**Function 1: Source Memory**
Every platform, hashtag, search term, and signal indicator is in `SOURCES/`. When you haven't scanned Korean platforms in 3 weeks, open `KOREAN_PLATFORMS.md` — fully briefed in 2 minutes. Nothing held in your head. Nothing lost between sessions.

**Function 2: Pattern Recognition Engine**
`CANDIDATES/FAILED/FAILURE_LOG.md` accumulates every rejection with the kill signal. After 20 entries it starts showing patterns. After 50 entries it becomes the fastest filter you have — you recognize the pattern before running any check. Feed the log to Claude periodically: "Here are my last 30 rejections and kill signals. What product type should I stop scanning for entirely?"

**Function 3: Intelligence Protection**
`INTELLIGENCE/WORKSHOP_ROSTER.md` and `SHOP_NETWORK.md` are private. The workshop knows their part. The shop knows their part. Only you see the whole map.

**Function 4: Prompt Library**
`PROMPTS/` contains every prompt and script you use repeatedly. The master ideation prompt evolves — when a constraint produces better outputs, update the file. The prompt library compounds in value exactly the way the scanning skill compounds — each iteration sharper than the last.

## THE SOURCE HIERARCHY IN PRACTICE

Not all sources are equal in signal strength. This is the order of production confidence:

| Rank | Source | Signal Type | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Kickstarter 500+ backers, live campaign | Proven demand, no supply anywhere | Strangers paid before it existed |
| 2 | Douyin 1M+ likes, 3–8 suppliers on 1688 | Proven viral, window open | Mass emotional response + manufacturing signal |
| 3 | r/somebodymakethis 500+ upvotes | Proven desire, no product exists | Global unmet need expressed directly |
| 4 | Yanko / Core77 high engagement | Proven aesthetic desire | Designers validated the concept |
| 5 | AI ideation (constrained prompt) | Hypothesis — no external validation | Strongest if passes pre-sell |
| 6 | Korean platforms 1,000+ reviews | Proven demand, different market | Needs Egyptian translation check |
| 7 | GitHub design files 100+ stars | Designer-validated, never produced | Producible immediately if concept fits |

Higher rank = shorter path to production confidence.
Lower rank = more pre-sell validation weight required before committing workshop time.

**The pre-sell step equates them all.** Regardless of source, 2 shop owner deposits is the final confirmation that overrides every rank.

Before any scanning session, confirm:

```
□ Which source am I scanning today?

□ Submit any survivors to Claude via DAILY_SCAN_SUBMISSION.md
```
